It’s hard to monetize your personal data for four reasons:

First, people who realize they can get money for their personal data would charge billions to those who use it, thus destabilizing the economy, unless second: the government controls the price floor and ceiling for the data, in which no one would agree with the low ceiling.

And third, people already give it away freely on social media and other outlets. Even if people could opt in to selling their data, because that’s how these companies would implement it, many people would never know the option existed, and only a small number of people would get paid for their data anyways assuming they were willing to give their bank account information to one of these companies. Of course the government would add this to taxable income so they would be all about it.

This leads me to my fourth and final point: with a small percentage of people actually requesting to get paid for their personal data, the data analytics companies would simply ignore them, reaping a profit off of the users that haven’t opted in. So those users looking for money still would not get paid.
